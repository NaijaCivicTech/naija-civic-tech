# NaijaCivicTech Vision

## What Is This?

NaijaCivicTech is an open source civic technology platform built by Nigerian developers, for Nigeria. It is not a link directory, a startup, or a funding pitch. It is a living platform where Nigerian problems get documented, understood, and attacked with working software, one tool at a time.

---

## The Problem We Are Solving

Nigerian developers are some of the most talented in the world. We build fintech products used across continents and ship tools that serve millions globally, but very little of that energy is pointed at home.

The civic tools that do exist for Nigeria are mostly closed source, underfunded, and built in isolation. They struggle to find users, fail to find institutional support, and eventually die quietly, with locked data, unverifiable methodology, and survival that depends on a single founder or donor.

The result is that Nigeria keeps starting from zero. Every election cycle, every infrastructure crisis, and every accountability effort begins without the foundation the last one built, because there is little institutional memory, no shared data layer, and no community that reliably outlasts any individual project. NaijaCivicTech exists to change that.

---

## The Vision

The aim is a single platform at naijacivictech.ng that Nigerians reach for whenever they need civic information or want to report a civic problem. Instead of many forgettable URLs, the goal is one platform people return to, one habit, and one open dataset that grows whenever someone reports an outage, rates a politician, flags a security incident, or casts a test vote.

The individual tools are useful on their own, but the real power is in what happens when their data lives together. Questions such as which constituencies combine the worst power supply with the least active representatives, which regions see the highest security incidents alongside the lowest government response rates, and which states show the largest gap between budget allocation and actual spending cannot be answered by a single tool; they need connected data. That connected intelligence is what NaijaCivicTech is building toward.

---

## Why Open Source

Every civic tool built on closed source infrastructure is one funding cut, one founder burnout, or one political pressure away from disappearing.

Open source means the community owns the work: data is auditable, methodology is transparent, and the platform can outlast any single contributor, sponsor, or administration. It also means Nigerian developers can learn from it, improve it, fork it, and build on top of it, so the codebase becomes as much a teaching resource as it is a product.

If we are serious about using technology to understand and solve Nigerian problems, the tools we build must meet the same transparency standards we demand from our institutions.

## The Platform Tools

NaijaCivicTech is built as a suite of focused tools that share a common platform, a common design language, and a common data layer.

### LightUp NG
A crowdsourced real time map of power outages across Nigeria by LGA. Citizens report outages and restorations, building a live picture of electricity reliability nationwide. Useful for researchers, journalists, prospective tenants and homebuyers making location decisions, and as evidence to hold DISCOs accountable.

### PolitiLog NG
A political ledger for every Nigerian politician from local government to federal level. Tracks tenure history, promises made, bills sponsored, attendance records, and citizen ratings. A living record that follows a politician across their entire career, maintained by the community, guided by verified sources. The institutional memory Nigerian democracy has never had.

### eVote NG
An open source digital voting prototype that simulates the full voting process: registration, biometric verification, ballot casting, tallying, and audit trail. It is not a replacement for INEC; it is a proof of concept that surfaces the real technical, security, and trust challenges of digital voting in Nigeria, built so the community can stress-test it, find the holes, and document what credible eVoting infrastructure would actually require.

### Bandit Report Tracker
A crowdsourced security incident map. Citizens report kidnappings, banditry, and community attacks by region. Data is aggregated, anonymised, and made public for researchers, journalists, and communities making safety decisions.

More tools will be added as the community identifies problems worth solving and contributors step up to build them.

## The Data Strategy

Each tool generates data, and the platform connects it.

Over time, NaijaCivicTech becomes the most comprehensive open civic dataset Nigeria has ever had: power reliability by LGA, politician performance by constituency, security incidents by region, and voting infrastructure stress tests by state.

This data will be:
- Publicly accessible via open APIs
- Exportable for researchers and journalists
- Cross-referenced across tools to surface insights no single dataset can provide
- Community maintained with clear contribution and verification standards

The goal is not only to build tools but to build the data infrastructure that makes Nigerian civic life legible, searchable, and impossible to ignore.

---

## The Phasing Plan

Trying to build everything at once is how projects die before they ship anything. NaijaCivicTech follows a strict phasing discipline.

### Phase 1 - Foundation
Ship two tools completely with real users and real data before touching anything else.

- LightUp NG, the power outage map
- PolitiLog NG, the political ledger

These two are chosen because they have immediate everyday use cases that are not tied to election cycles: a Nigerian can use LightUp on any given Tuesday, and a voter can look up a politician any day of the year, which builds the habit of coming to the platform before the higher stakes tools arrive.

### Phase 2 - Expand
Once Phase 1 has real users, active contributors, and a stable data foundation:

- eVote NG prototype
- Bandit Report Tracker
- Community suggested tools that pass the acceptance criteria review

### Phase 3 - Connect
Surface cross-tool intelligence and build the data layer that connects outage maps to politician records to security incidents. This is where NaijaCivicTech becomes something that cannot be replicated by any single closed source tool.

## What This Is Not

**Not a startup.** There is no equity, pitch deck, or exit strategy. If the project becomes financially sustainable through grants or community support, that money goes back into the platform.

**Not a government project.** NaijaCivicTech is independent: it does not seek government validation before building or require institutional approval to exist. If agencies eventually engage with the platform or its data, that should be a consequence of traction, not a precondition for building.

**Not a replacement for policy.** Technology does not fix human behaviour or corrupt institutions on its own. NaijaCivicTech removes information barriers and documents what is happening; what Nigerians do with better information is their choice, and the platform’s job is to supply the information.

**Not finished.** This document will evolve as the platform evolves. Decisions here are starting points, not commandments.

---

## The Approach to Institutions

NaijaCivicTech does not fight agencies; the fight is with the problem, not the institution.

If NAFDAC, INEC, or any other agency eventually engages with what we build, the conversation changes because we are not pitching an idea—we are showing them something Nigerians already depend on. Quiet traction opens doors that public pressure often closes.

---

## Who Can Contribute

Everyone is welcome, whether or not they are Nigerian or write code.

- Developers can build tools, fix bugs, review pull requests
- Designers can improve the platform experience
- Domain experts, doctors, farmers, lawyers, teachers, can stress test tools for real world accuracy
- Citizens can report data, rate politicians, flag inaccuracies
- Researchers and journalists can use and cite the data, and feed findings back into the platform
- Policy people can document the implementation barriers so builders understand the full landscape

The only requirement is that contributions serve the mission: build for Nigeria, in the open, with receipts.

---

## The Principle We Build By

Nigeria has no shortage of problems or of talented people. What it has lacked is a shared surface where both meet, in the open, with the work visible to everyone.

That is what NaijaCivicTech is building: one tool at a time, with many contributors, in the open, without waiting for permission.
